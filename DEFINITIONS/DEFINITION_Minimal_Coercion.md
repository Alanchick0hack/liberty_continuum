# DEFINITION — Minimal Coercion

## Definition
Minimal coercion is the principle that force and compulsion should be used only where legitimate and necessary, and otherwise replaced by voluntary participation, transparent systems, and accessible services.

## What qualifies as legitimate coercion
- Rights enforcement
- Courts and dispute arbitration
- Protection against violence, fraud, and corruption
- Limited emergency powers under strict oversight

## What it rejects
- Coercion as convenience
- Hidden enforcement
- Bureaucracy as punishment
- Compliance systems that expand by default
